Call 999 if you have any of these:

  • you hurt your head badly, e.g. deep wound from a fall or accident
  • your headache came on suddenly and is very painful (like being hit with a brick)
  • your headache is like the worst pain you’ve ever had and came on very suddenly, especially after: coughing, laughing, sneezing, changing posture or exercising
  • you suddenly have problems speaking, can’t remember things, feel drowsy, confused
  • You have a severe headache and:
    • sudden problems speaking, remembering things
    • blurred vision
    • feeling drowsy, confused
    • high fever
    • stiff neck
    • rash
    • redness in one of your eyes
    • jaw pain when chewing

Treating headaches at home

Most headaches will go away on their own and aren’t a sign of something more serious.

You can simply wait until the headache goes away or get painkillers like paracetamol or ibuprofen at the pharmacy.

This will also help with your headache:

  • drink enough water
  • eat regular meals
  • sleep the same amount of hours every night

Headaches can last between 30 minutes and several hours.

You can get headaches because of something else, eg cold, flu, bad posture. Stress and life events that cause stress can also be a reason why you have headaches.

Some women get headaches when they’re on the pill or during their period or menopause.

When to see your GP

See your GP if:

  • you have a bad throbbing pain at the front or side of your head
  • you feel sick, vomit and find light or noise painful
  • you see flashing lights when the headache starts
