Check how bad it is and how long you’ve had it for

Stomach pain can feel different for everyone. You might describe it as:

  • sharp
  • stabbing
  • crampy
  • colicky
  • dull

Check if you have anything else with your stomach ache (see below) and what to do about it.

Often stomach pain goes away on its own after a few hours.

If it doesn’t or it gets much worse in a short space of time, you should see your GP.

You feel bloated or can’t go to the toilet

You can get stomach pain when:

  • you feel bloated
  • your body finds it hard to digest food (indigestion)
  • you can’t go to the toilet (constipation)

You can get medicine from your pharmacy to help with these problems.

Some foods might make you feel bloated or give you indigestion. Write down what you eat every day to find out what might make you feel worse. Cut down on those foods.

Movement can help with constipation, eg walking faster than normal for 20 minutes a day.

You are vomiting or have diarrhoea

If you have stomach ache and are also vomiting or have diarrhoea, you might have a tummy bug or food poisoning. This usually goes away after a few days.  

If it doesn’t go away or you feel worse, see your GP.

You’ve had stomach ache for a while

There can be many reasons for this and you should go and see your GP.
