Call 999 if you have any of these:

  • sudden, bad stomach pain
  • pain when you touch your stomach
  • vomiting blood
  • blood in your poo
  • you collapsed
  • can’t breathe
  • not peeing at all
  • you have an ongoing serious illness
  • you’re diabetic

When to see your GP

Children and babies with bad stomach ache that won’t go away should always see the GP.

You should see your GP if:

  • the pain gets much worse in a short space of time
  • you have the pain for several days
  • you also have other symptoms like loss of weight, vomiting, or abnormal bleeding

Things you can treat at home

Your pharmacist can give you medicine to help with:

  • feeling bloated
  • indigestion - feeling full, heavy, sick, maybe bringing up food
  • constipation

Vomiting and diarrhoea

You might also vomit or have diarrhoea with your stomach ache.

This isn’t usually anything serious and will go away after a few days. If it doesn’t or you feel much worse, you should consult your GP
